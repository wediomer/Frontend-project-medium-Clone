Extending the Front-End Engineer role

How to contribute outside your zone while being inside it

Khaled Al-Ansari
min read Jul 5

Front end development had a massive rise in the past 5–6 years especially with the introduction of many great things in it like ES6, NPM, Node.js, and cool frameworks like React and Angular, but all of this somehow still didn’t help to change the mentality of any Front End Engineer (FEE) unfortunately and that’s why many are stuck rounding in the same circle.

Let’s be honest, as an FEE you are already struggling with people thinking you are a Web Designer and people still need to know the difference to understand you work. But seriously distinguishing yourself from Web Designers might be the first step in a career full of teammates who don’t know your job, the second step is to contribute more and help them understand your needs and this is where extending you role is a must!

Yes some web client side application require an experienced person to handle some serious interactions like interactive games with all that graphics in it or financial apps with all of the real time details that comes with it shown in all of the charts across the app. Though not all FEEs will work on such stuff and because of that they need to know some stuff that considered ambiguous for teammates who are helping delivering the front-end application since their point of view of the work is different but at the end the output is related to the UI and other engineers tend not to like dealing with UI or anything close to it and that’s where you come in champ!
